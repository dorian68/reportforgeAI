# ReportForge AI Business Plan

## Product Thesis

ReportForge AI should be sold as an Excel-first reporting automation product for people who already live in spreadsheets and repeatedly turn raw Excel data into executive reporting artifacts.

The strongest core promise is:

`Select a range in Excel -> generate a board-ready workbook report, executive summary, email draft, and slide outline in minutes.`

This is stronger than selling it as a generic "AI add-in". Buyers pay for time saved, consistency, and polish, not for AI branding alone.

## Best Initial Markets

### 1. FP&A Analysts

Why this market fits:

- high Excel usage
- recurring monthly and quarterly reporting cycles
- strong pain around turning tables into management packs
- clear ROI if the tool saves even 1-2 hours per month

What matters most:

- one-click report refresh
- executive summary templates
- KPI cards and board-style Excel output
- slide-ready and email-ready outputs

Why they may pay:

- a single analyst saving 2 hours per month can justify a $29 monthly spend easily

### 2. Finance Managers And CFO Office Teams

Why this market fits:

- need consistent reporting packs for leadership
- often work with unstable Excel-based reporting processes
- value narrative quality and formatting consistency

What matters most:

- trustworthy workbook output
- reusable templates by audience
- controlled AI usage
- review-ready outputs for executive communication

Why they may pay:

- reporting quality and speed both matter
- team plans are more realistic than single-seat pricing here

### 3. RevOps And Business Operations Teams

Why this market fits:

- frequently build operational reviews from exports and spreadsheet data
- need KPI communication more than deep BI infrastructure
- often operate in fast-moving SMB and mid-market environments

What matters most:

- repeatable KPI reporting
- charts and summaries that are easy to share
- weekly and monthly refresh workflows

Why they may pay:

- the tool helps turn messy data exports into leadership-facing updates quickly

### 4. Consultants And Agencies Building Recurring Client Reports

Why this market fits:

- repeated client reporting is a strong automation use case
- consultants already monetize time, so time savings have direct monetary value
- one user can manage multiple clients and justify subscription cost immediately

What matters most:

- template libraries
- client-specific report styles
- export quality
- low-friction onboarding

Why they may pay:

- this is one of the strongest early monetization segments for a $29 Pro plan

### 5. PE Portfolio Ops / Board Reporting For SMB And Mid-Market Companies

Why this market fits:

- recurring board and lender reporting is high-value and deadline-driven
- many portfolio companies still rely heavily on Excel
- board-style summary generation is a meaningful differentiator

What matters most:

- high-trust executive formatting
- board pack templates
- traceable KPI logic
- strong Excel and slide outputs

Why they may pay:

- this can support higher-value team and business plans if the workflow is stable

## Markets To Avoid First

### 1. Large Regulated Enterprises As First Customers

Avoid first because:

- procurement cycles are slow
- security and compliance requirements are much higher
- the current product still needs backend controls, auditability, and legal packaging

### 2. Generic Consumers

Avoid first because:

- the product is too specialized
- consumer willingness to pay is weak
- onboarding/support cost would be disproportionate

### 3. Data Science Teams

Avoid first because:

- they already have more flexible tooling
- the add-in value proposition is weaker for them

### 4. Google-Native Organizations As Primary Target

Avoid first because:

- the product is fundamentally an Excel / Office Add-in
- Microsoft-first outputs are a better fit for the default buyer

Google integrations should remain optional connectors, not the main market story.

## Product Positioning

Primary positioning:

- Excel-native reporting automation for finance and ops teams

Secondary positioning:

- board-pack and executive reporting copilot

Do not lead with:

- generic AI assistant
- Google Apps Script as the headline capability
- experimentation / creativity messaging

Lead with:

- time saved
- consistency
- executive-ready deliverables
- lower reporting friction

## What Must Be True For A $29 Plan To Feel Worth It

The product must consistently save the user at least 1-2 hours per month.

The minimum value stack for a $29 Pro plan is:

- stable Excel report generation
- reusable templates
- one-click refresh workflow
- email and slide companion outputs
- onboarding that produces a result in under 10 minutes
- clear error handling
- optional AI enhancement that genuinely improves narrative quality

If the product still feels like a prototype, $29 will feel expensive.

If the product feels like a dependable monthly reporting assistant, $29 becomes easy to justify.

## Recommended Pricing Model

### Pro

- $29 per user per month
- single user
- local templates
- optional BYO LLM provider
- core Excel/email/slides outputs

### Team

- $99-$149 per month
- 3-5 seats
- shared templates
- team workspace settings
- admin controls
- basic usage analytics

### Business

- $299+ per month
- departmental rollout
- admin deployment support
- audit logs
- tenant policy controls
- SSO
- managed support

### Enterprise

- annual contract
- security review
- custom onboarding
- procurement support
- custom SLAs

Institution-level buyers should not be forced into a $29-only pricing model.

## Revenue Math

To reach $10,000 MRR:

- at $29/month: about 345 active subscriptions
- at $99/month: about 101 active subscriptions
- at $149/month: about 68 active subscriptions
- at $299/month: about 34 active subscriptions

The fastest path is usually:

- Pro for individuals and consultants
- Team for small finance and ops groups
- Business for the best customers who need admin and trust features

## Shortest Path To Revenue

### Best Early Buyer Types

- independent consultants
- boutique finance / FP&A advisory firms
- SMB finance managers
- RevOps leads in growing B2B companies

These buyers:

- already suffer from recurring Excel reporting work
- can buy faster than large enterprises
- can validate whether the product is truly worth paying for

### Best Acquisition Motions

- direct outreach with demo workbook examples
- short demo video showing a raw Excel range becoming a board-ready pack
- founder-led sales to design partners
- templates targeted to finance and ops use cases

### Best Proof Of Value

- hours saved per monthly reporting cycle
- time-to-first-board-pack
- reduced manual formatting work
- higher reporting consistency across cycles

## Institution-Ready Roadmap

### Phase 1. Sharpen The Core Product

- Excel workbook report must be excellent every time
- add one-click refresh from the same workbook/table
- improve native PowerPoint and Outlook alignment
- reduce the importance of optional connectors in the product story

### Phase 2. Add Commercial Infrastructure

- billing and subscription management
- licensing and entitlement checks
- free trial and upgrade path
- support workflow
- usage analytics

### Phase 3. Add Trust Infrastructure

- privacy policy
- terms of service
- DPA
- security page
- audit logging
- error monitoring
- incident response process

### Phase 4. Add Enterprise Controls

- Microsoft SSO
- admin deployment path
- shared templates
- team settings
- policy controls for AI and connectors
- managed secret handling through a backend control plane

### Phase 5. Add Certification And Procurement Readiness

- Publisher Attestation
- Microsoft 365 Certification target
- penetration testing
- security questionnaire pack
- architecture and data-flow diagrams

## Metrics To Track

### Product Metrics

- activation rate
- time to first successful report
- report refresh frequency
- percentage of users who create outputs beyond Excel
- error rate per host and feature

### Revenue Metrics

- trial to paid conversion
- paid retention
- expansion from Pro to Team or Business
- churn by customer segment
- CAC payback

### Value Metrics

- self-reported hours saved
- number of recurring templates created
- number of monthly reports refreshed from prior templates

## What To Cut Or Deprioritize

- overemphasis on Google workflows for the default buyer
- novelty "variation" messaging as a core value proposition
- broad market targeting too early
- enterprise sales before trust and admin controls exist

## Recommended Narrative For The Product

Use this story:

`ReportForge AI helps finance and operations teams turn Excel data into executive-ready reports, faster and with less manual work.`

Avoid this story:

`ReportForge AI is an all-purpose AI spreadsheet assistant.`

The first story sells a workflow.
The second story sells a vague category.

## Final Business Recommendation

The best path is:

1. win consultants, FP&A analysts, finance managers, and RevOps leads first
2. prove repeat usage with recurring reporting workflows
3. charge $29 for Pro, but introduce higher team and business tiers early
4. move toward institution-level readiness only after trust, controls, and backend infrastructure exist

That is the most realistic path to making this product both credible and commercially durable.
